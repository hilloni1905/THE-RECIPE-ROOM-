Rabdi Jalebi

Rabdi jalebi is a popular Indian dessert combination. "Rabdi" is a sweet, condensed milk-based dessert, while "jalebi" is a deep-fried, spiral-shaped sweet made from a fermented batter soaked in sugar syrup. Together, they create a delightful contrast of flavors and textures – the creamy richness of rabdi complements the crispy and syrup-soaked jalebi. It's a beloved treat often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions in India.

Prep Time

30-35 mins

Cook Time

approx 1.5 hours

Serving

2 servings

Instructions

For Jalebi

Step 1

In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornstarch, yogurt, and baking soda. Mix well to form a smooth batter. Add saffron strands for color (if desired). Let the batter rest for 30 minutes.

Step 2

In a deep frying pan or kadai, heat enough oil for deep frying. Keep the heat on medium-low.

Step 3

Transfer the jalebi batter to a squeeze bottle or a piping bag. Squeeze the batter into the hot oil in a circular, coiled pattern. Fry until the jalebis turn golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.

Step 4

Remove the fried jalebis from the oil and drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.

Step 5

In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, saffron strands, and cardamom powder. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5-7 minutes until the syrup slightly thickens. Keep it warm.


For Rabdi

Step 1

In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat the milk and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the milk reduces by about half and thickens. This process may take about 30-40 minutes.

Step 2

Add sugar, cardamom powder, saffron strands, and chopped nuts. Continue to simmer for another 5-7 minutes. Stir in rose water (if desired) and remove from heat. Allow it to cool.

Step 3

To serve, place the crispy jalebis in the warm sugar syrup for a few seconds, then remove them. Serve the jalebis with a generous serving of chilled rabdi on top.

Ingredients
For Jalebi:

1 cup all-purpose flour

1 tablespoon cornstarch

1/2 cup yogurt

A pinch of baking soda

1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)

A few strands of saffron (optional, for color)

Oil, for frying


For Sugar Syrup:

1 cup granulated sugar

1/2 cup water

A few saffron strands

1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder


For Rabdi:

2 cups full-fat milk

1/4 cup granulated sugar

A pinch of cardamom powder

A few saffron strands

A handful of chopped nuts (like almonds and pistachios)

1/2 teaspoon rose water (optional)
