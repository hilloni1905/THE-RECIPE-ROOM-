Tiramisu

Tiramisu is a popular Italian dessert made with layers of coffee-soaked sponge cake (ladyfingers) and a creamy mixture of mascarpone cheese, eggs, sugar, and cocoa powder. It's typically chilled and dusted with cocoa powder on top.

Prep Time

20-30 min.

Chilling Time

4 hours minimum

Serving

1 to 2 servings

Instructions

Step 1

In a shallow dish, combine the cooled coffee and coffee liqueur (if using).

Step 2

In a heatproof bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and whisk constantly until the mixture thickens and becomes pale in color (about 5 minutes).

Step 3

Remove from heat and let it cool.

Step 4

In a separate bowl, beat the mascarpone cheese until smooth.

Step 5

In another bowl, whip the heavy cream and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.

Step 6

Gently fold the mascarpone cheese into the egg yolk mixture, then fold in the whipped cream until well combined.

Step 7

Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture (don't soak them) and arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a serving dish.

Step 8

Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.

Step 9

Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone mixture.

Step 10

Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight.

Step 11

Before serving, dust the top with cocoa powder and garnish with chocolate shavings if desired.

Ingredients

1 cup brewed strong coffee, cooled

3 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)

4 large egg yolks

3/4 cup granulated sugar

1 cup mascarpone cheese

1 1/2 cups heavy cream

1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

1 package ladyfingers

Unsweetened cocoa powder for dusting

Semi-sweet chocolate shavings (optional)
