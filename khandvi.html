Khandvi

Khandvi, also known as Suralichi Vadi or Patuli, is a popular Gujarati snack from India. It is a savory dish made from a batter consisting of gram flour (besan) and buttermilk. The batter is cooked on a stovetop until it thickens and then spread into thin layers, which are rolled up into cylindrical shapes once they cool down. These rolls are then tempered with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and other spices.

Prep Time

10 min.

Cook Time

20 min.

Serving

2 servings

Instructions

Step 1

In a bowl, add gram flour, curd, turmeric powder, red chilli powder and salt to taste.

Step 2

In another bowl, add 2 cups of water and ginger paste mix well.

Step 3

Add little by little water into the bowl and whisk well.

Step 4

Make sure there is no lump to the batter.

Step 5

In a kadai, add the mixture and cook stirring continuously till it becomes a smooth thick batter on medium flame.

Step 6

Quickly spread the mixture over the greased thalis as thinly as possible. while the batter is still hot.

Step 7

When cool, cut into strips two inches wide and sprinkle some grated coconut, coriander leaves and roll them tightly.

For Tempering

Step 1

Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, curry leaves let it splutter.

Step 2

Add asafoetida, green chillies and salt to taste.

Step 3

Pour over the prepared khandvi and serve hot.

Ingredients
For Khandvi Batter:

1 cup Gram flour

1 cup Curd

2 cup Water

1 tsp Ginger paste

1 tsp Turmeric powder

½ tsp Red chilli powder

Salt to taste

For Filling:

4 tbsp fresh Coconut, grated

2 tbsp Coriander leaves, finely chopped

For Tempering:

3 tsp Oil

1 tsp Mustard seeds

1 tsp Sesame seed

1 sprig Curry leaves, chopped

¼ tsp Asafoetida

2 tablespoons Oil + 2 tablespoons Butter

Butter for serving

2 Green chilli, slit,

Salt to taste
